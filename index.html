Xingyao Wu

Tenure-Track Professor, Shandong University

I am a tenure-track professor specializing in quantum information science, quantum circuit optimization, and physics-informed quantum algorithms. My work spans the boundaries of fundamental theoretical physics and applied quantum machine learning, with a specific focus on scaling quantum simulations and designing highly optimized software frameworks.

Previously, I served as a project lead and senior research scientist at JD Explore Academy, where I led the design of the open-source quantum machine learning framework TeD-Q. I completed my postdoctoral fellowship at the Joint Center for Quantum Information and Computer Science (QuICS), University of Maryland, following my Ph.D. from the Centre for Quantum Technologies (CQT), National University of Singapore.

Research Fields

Quantum Machine Learning

Developing and accelerating hybrid quantum-classical algorithms, neural network architectures, and differentiable programming pipelines.

Circuit Optimization

Leveraging tensor networks to scale quantum circuit simulations, optimize large-scale VQE (Variational Quantum Eigensolver), and minimize physical gate overhead.

Quantum Foundations

Investigating quantum nonlocality, device-independent self-testing protocols, and multipartite entanglement characterization.
